# AVPlayer over-downshift on descending cap — 47d16786 — 2026-06-12

## Summary
On a **descending** bandwidth cap, iOS AVPlayer **over-downshifts** — it drops
**≥2 variant rungs at once** (skipping the rung that matches the new cap), then
**corrects upward** a rung once it re-measures headroom. This happens with a
**full, healthy buffer (~28 s)**, so it is estimator caution, *not* rebuffer
avoidance. The **ascent is clean** — the same player ladders monotonically
through *every* rung on the way up. The descent-only asymmetry is the whole
point: rung-skipping is not a general trait, it is specifically a downshift
overshoot. Confirmed on iPhone15,4 / iOS 26.5 and reproduced in historic sim
pyramids. Tag: **confirmed**.

## Hypothesis (confirmed)
AVPlayer's throughput estimator **overshoots downward** on each descending cap
step: it undershoots the new sustainable rate, drops one rung too far, then
upshifts to correct after re-measuring against a full buffer. It is *not*
buffer-driven (buffer full throughout) and *not* a fast-transition artifact (the
descent is slow, 16–60 s per cap step). The behaviour is a stable AVPlayer trait
across **iPhone (26.5) and sims (26.4)** — the *behaviour* is consistent; *which
session over-downshoots at which band* is stochastic (two byte-identical sims,
same OS/content/caps, landed on opposite sides of the 1440p band in one run).

## Caveat — leader-gated cadence (magnitude only, not existence)
This run used the **early-exit** step driver, which advances each step when the
**leader** (a sim) settles, then broadcasts that cadence to all members. So the
iPhone's steps were timed by a *faster-settling sim* — cap drops arrived before
the iPhone fully settled, which can **inflate** its over-downshift/hunting
*count*. The over-downshift *behaviour* is still real (full-buffer overshoot,
present in historic data too), but the iPhone's apparent *severity* relative to
the sims is confounded until re-measured under a **constant** step duration
(`CHAR_STEP_S`, added 2026-06-12 in `modes/sweep.go:holdWithEarlyExit` — fixed
dwell, early-exit disabled, so all members get identical cap AND duration).
